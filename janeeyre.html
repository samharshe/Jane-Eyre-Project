John Reed is an angry, spoiled brat. He bullies Jane and makes sure that she never enjoys herself at all, but karma catches up when he blows his mom's money on wasteful vices. In short, he's exactly the type of person you'd expect to end up in a Kendrick Lamar diss track.
Next: Mr. Brocklehurst

Mr. Brocklehurst

"Perception is reality depends how they receive you.
Anyone can sell a story it depends if they believe you."
- BoB

Mr. Brocklehurst is a sanctimonious hypocrite who tries to make everyone believe that he is upright and altruistic. He embarrasses Jane by making her stand on a stool in front of the class, and generally makes sure that Lowood School is miserable for everyone. Mr. Brocklehurst really took this BoB line to heart, but, unfortunately, nobody is buying his story.
Next: Mr. Rochester

Mr. Rochester

"I'm in love with you,

"I'm in love with you."

Jane and Mr. Rochester have the type of complicated relationship that you would expect to find described in a Kanye West song. At first, Mr. Rochester hides the fact that he is already married from Jane, leaving her with emotional scars. In the end, they get back together in a love story even better than Kim K and Kanye's.
Next: St. John Rivers

St. John Rivers

"I got fake people
Showin' fake love to me
Straight up to my face!"
- Drake

St. John Rivers is a plain but upright man. He badly wants to marry Jane, but his love is clearly fake; he only wants her because he thinks she would be a good missionary. Jane finds herself wanting to please him, but she eventually becomes annoyed with his persistent marriage proposals. She sees through his requests: He only loves his God and his Bible, I'm sorry.
